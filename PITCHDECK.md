# CityBus — Pitch Deck

## Mass Events, Zero Fleet Visibility

### The Coordination Crisis RCCG Faces at Redemption City

At Redemption City mega-events, free interstate bus fleets are run on phone calls and instinct, with no live map of buses, capacity, or seat availability, so attendees get stranded while half-empty buses leave nearby pickup points. Free bus coordination across interstate routes becomes a critical logistical failure point. The people experiencing this problem are RCCG program coordinators, bus drivers, and attendees traveling from different states across Nigeria. Current workarounds are deeply insufficient.



---

## Real-Time Fleet Command, One Platform

### How CityBus Gives RCCG Full Visibility at Redemption City

CityBus puts every interstate shuttle on one live map—location, capacity, and routes—for coordinators, drivers, and attendees. Deployed in days from drivers’ phones, it replaces phone coordination with real-time fleet control at Redemption City.

## Business Design & Go-To-Market — Positioning, Revenue, and Distribution

### Market positioning

CityBus is the **real-time fleet command layer for mass-event and faith-based shuttle networks**—starting with RCCG convention-scale interstate coordination at Redemption City, then expanding to similar high-volume programs (crusades, regional gatherings, university convocations, municipal event shuttles).

**Positioning statement:** *The modern alternative to phone-tree logistics for fleets of 10–500+ buses where legacy civic AVL systems are too slow, too expensive, and not built for temporary, multi-state volunteer operations.*

### Target user demographic

| Segment | Role | Primary need |
|---------|------|----------------|
| **Program / transport coordinators** | RCCG command center, logistics leads | Fleet-wide visibility, deployment control, analytics |
| **Bus drivers & fleet captains** | Volunteer and contracted drivers | Simple shift tools, GPS sync, passenger counts |
| **Attendees & state delegations** | Interstate travelers | Know which bus has space before leaving the pickup point |

**Initial wedge:** RCCG national and zonal transport teams for Convention, Holy Ghost Congress, and other Redemption City programs drawing millions.

### Initial target customers & distribution channels

| Phase | Customers | Channels |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **1 — Beachhead** | RCCG transport / protocol units (national convention, Redemption Camp operations) | Direct pilot with convention logistics leadership; demos at coordinator pre-event briefings; referral through existing driver and state liaison networks |
| **2 — Adjacent** | Other large Nigerian ministries and event organizers running free interstate shuttles | Partnerships with event logistics vendors; word-of-mouth from RCCG pilot case study |
| **3 — Platform** | Universities, corporate campuses, small municipal shuttle programs | Direct B2B outreach; municipal and campus RFPs; integrators serving local government IT |

### Monetization strategy

| Stream | Model |
|--------|--------|
| **SaaS — Base** | Monthly fee per active bus/driver: live tracking, admin console, driver portal, basic analytics |
| **SaaS — Enterprise** | Per-event or annual license: advanced analytics, API access, white-label passenger app, multi-route templates for recurring programs |
| **Services** | One-time onboarding (routes, roles, fleet import) and optional SLA support during peak event windows |

**Sustainable economics:** Revenue scales with fleet size and event calendar; marginal cost stays low on Supabase/serverless stack so pricing undercuts legacy AVL while remaining profitable at 50–500 bus deployments.

**Cost-saving for customers:** Less fuel waste from half-empty runs, fewer stranded attendees (reduced ad-hoc rescue trips), and coordinator time reclaimed from hours of phone coordination—measurable ROI per convention cycle.

### Unique competitive advantage vs. legacy civic alternatives

| Legacy approach | CityBus advantage |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Months-long procurement and on-prem AVL installs | **Deploy in days** — browser-based, no dedicated hardware beyond drivers’ phones |
| Built for fixed municipal routes, not surge volunteer fleets | **Event-native** — rapid route setup, role-based access, real-time passenger counts |
| Expensive licenses and IT maintenance | **Serverless cost structure** — pay for usage, attractive for ministry and NGO budgets |
| Clunky operator UIs with low driver adoption | **Modern UX** — React + live maps; three portals tuned for coordinator, driver, and attendee |
| No seat-level visibility for informal free fleets | **Capacity awareness** — board/alight telemetry feeds admin and passenger views |
